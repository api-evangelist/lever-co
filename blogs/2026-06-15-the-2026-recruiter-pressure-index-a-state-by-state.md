Lever's analysis argues that while national hiring statistics look calm, hiring pressure varies dramatically by state, with a nearly fivefold spread between the tightest and loosest environments. South Dakota ranks first with a score of 82.1, while California (50th) and Washington D.C. (51st) face the opposite dynamic of more candidates than roles. The takeaway: hiring strategy is increasingly a local question despite identical national metrics.
